SAFETY TIPS

Please find some of the useful tips to assist on safe usage and living around electricity:
  1. Fallen or hanging electricity conductors:

    All should be assumed to be live thus; do not come close or touch them instead inform the nearest TANESCO office or call center.

  2. Tree near or under electricity power lines:

    Tree are good conductors of electricity therefore whether wanted to cut or prune a tree that is under or close to electricity power lines first contact nearest TANESCO office to facilitate a safe exercise.

  3. Stay wire:

    Stay wire can sometime be electrified by the live conductors therefore should not be touched or allow children to play around.

WARNING: If someone comes into contact with a live electrical source - do not touch. Some tips on how to avoid electrical and fire accidents
  1. Do not do any activity, carry long objects or/and build under electrical power lines.
  2. Stop vandalism of electrical equipment.
  3. Do not set bush fires.
  4. Objects touched by a fallen wire e.g. fences, buildings or even surrounding ground must be considered energized and should not be touched.
  5. Stay away from towers and lines during extreme windstorms, heavy rains, thunderstorms or other extreme conditions.
  6. Do not use VISHOKA on any electrical work. Utilize your nearest TANESCO Office.
  7. Do not cut trees or prune tree branches close or under electrical power lines
  8. Do not touch or play around stay wires.
How to avoid fire accident in buildings?
  1. Employ authorized/licensed electrical installation contractors during wiring installation your building. Do not extend electrical wiring to the front house or chicken hut without using licensed contractor
  2. Inspect/check wiring installations in your building every five (5) years using licensed contractor
  3. Secure any loose connection whenever happens using certified electrician.
  4. Avoid overloading your sockets by plugging many appliances at one socket
  5. Test electrical protection devices e.g. residual current device (RCD) by pressing the testing button at least once in a month
If someone comes into contact with a live electrical source - do not touch.
  1. Push him or her away using bad conductors of electricity e.g. a piece of dry wood. Be very careful as you could as well endanger your life.
  2. Inform nearby TANESCO office for any electrical related accident, please inform the nearby TANESCO office.
